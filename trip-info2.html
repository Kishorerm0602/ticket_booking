Best Seasons to Travel

India is beautiful year-round — each season has its own magic

☀️

Summer

March – May

Perfect for hill stations, Himalayan treks, and Northeast India. Escape the plains heat in Manali, Spiti, and Sikkim.

🌧️

Monsoon

June – September

Kerala, Coorg, and the Northeast come alive with waterfalls and lush greenery. Fewer crowds, lower prices, raw beauty.

🍂

Autumn

October – November

The golden sweet spot — cool and clear skies everywhere. Rajasthan, Goa, and South India are at their glorious best.

❄️

Winter

December – February

Peak season for Rajasthan, Andaman, Kerala beaches, and Rann of Kutch. Festivals, fairs, and comfortable temperatures.

Travel Tips

Insider advice from our team of seasoned Indian travel experts

💸

Cash is still king

Rural India and hill stations often have poor connectivity. Always carry sufficient cash and keep some small change handy for temple offerings and local markets.

🕌

Respect local customs

Remove footwear at temples and mosques. Dress modestly at religious sites. Always ask before photographing locals — a smile and a nod go a long way.

🚰

Drink only packaged water

Stick to sealed packaged water throughout your trip. Carry oral rehydration salts — useful for heat, long journeys, and adjusting to new cuisine.

📱

Download offline maps

Save Google Maps areas offline before your trip. In Ladakh, Northeast, and remote Himachal, network coverage can be near-zero for stretches.

🚂

Book trains early

Popular routes like Chennai–Bangalore, Delhi–Jaipur, and Mumbai–Goa sell out weeks in advance. Book on IRCTC as soon as your dates are confirmed.

🌅

Start early, every day

India's best sights — the Taj Mahal, Amber Fort, backwater sunrises — are magical at dawn before crowds arrive. Rise early and you'll be rewarded.

🎒

Pack light, travel light

A 7kg cabin bag is enough for most 7-day trips. Clothes can be washed or bought. Heavy bags slow you down on temple steps and mountain roads.

🌶️

Be adventurous with food

Don't just eat at hotel restaurants. Seek out local dhabas, filter coffee shops, and street carts with long queues — they're usually the best meals of the trip.

🏔️

Acclimatise in altitude

Heading to Ladakh or Spiti? Spend at least two days resting after you arrive before trekking or exerting yourself. Altitude sickness is serious — don't rush.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most from our travellers

How do I book a package with Wanderlust India?

Simply visit our Contact page and fill in your details — destination, travel month, group size, and any special preferences. Our team will reach out within 24 hours with a customised itinerary and quote. You can also call us directly at +91 98400 00000.

What is the cancellation policy?

Cancellations made 30+ days before departure receive a full refund minus a 5% processing fee. 15–29 days: 50% refund. Less than 15 days: no refund. We strongly recommend purchasing travel insurance to cover unforeseen cancellations.

Can I customise a package for my group?

Absolutely — in fact, most of our bookings are customised. You can choose your destinations, duration, accommodation category, meal preferences, and pace. Just tell us your dream trip and we'll build it around you. Groups of 10+ receive special pricing.

Are flights included in the package price?

Base package prices do not include domestic flights. However, we can arrange and book flights for you at competitive rates. Packages marked "Flights Included" in the tag already have air travel bundled in — the price you see is all-inclusive.

Is Wanderlust India safe for solo travellers and women?

Yes. Safety is our top priority. All our drivers, guides, and accommodation partners are thoroughly vetted. We offer women-only group tours on select routes. Our 24/7 helpline is always available. Solo female travellers make up a significant portion of our guests, and their feedback is consistently positive.

Do I need a permit for Ladakh or Northeast India?

Yes — certain areas in Ladakh (Nubra Valley, Pangong Lake) and states like Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, and Nagaland require Inner Line Permits (ILP) for Indian citizens. We arrange all necessary permits as part of your package — you just need to share your ID documents in advance.

What's the best time to visit from Chennai specifically?

From Chennai, October to March is ideal for most destinations. Summer (April–June) is perfect for Himachal Pradesh and Ladakh. Monsoon (June–August) is spectacular for Kerala, Coorg, and Meghalaya. We're happy to suggest the best route for any month you have free.
