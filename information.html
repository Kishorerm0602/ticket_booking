France, officially the French Republic, is the world's most visited country, attracting 95 million international tourists annually.[1] Known for its iconic landmarks, world-class cuisine, and rich cultural heritage, France offers diverse experiences from the romantic streets of Paris to the lavender fields of Provence and the medieval abbey of Mont Saint-Michel.

Bangkok, officially Krung Thep Maha Nakhon (City of Angels), is Thailand's capital and Southeast Asia's most visited city, welcoming 22.7 million international tourists annually.[4] Known as the "Venice of the East," Bangkok blends golden temples, floating markets, skyscrapers, and street food across the Chao Phraya River delta, offering spiritual serenity alongside urban excitement.

Sydney, capital of New South Wales, is Australia's largest city and global icon, renowned for its sail-shaped Sydney Opera House and towering Harbour Bridge. With 20.7 million annual visitors, Sydney blends world-class beaches (Bondi), natural harbour beauty, and vibrant multicultural districts across Port Jackson, consistently ranking among the world's most liveable cities.

The Maldives, an archipelago of 1,190 coral islands across 26 atolls in the Indian Ocean, is the world's premier luxury beach destination, welcoming 2.25 million tourists annually to its overwater bungalows and turquoise lagoons. Renowned for pristine marine biodiversity, white-sand beaches, and record-breaking resort luxury, the Maldives offers unparalleled underwater experiences including manta rays, whale sharks, and vibrant coral reefs.

Major Tourist Attractions

152+ luxury resorts with glass-floor villas directly above coral reefs. Infinity pools, private decks, direct ocean access.

Pristine white sandbars accessible by dhoni boat. Snorkeling paradise with sea turtles and reef sharks.
